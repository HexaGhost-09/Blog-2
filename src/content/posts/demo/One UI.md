## One UI 9 Is Here — Built on Android 17

Samsung kicked off the **One UI 9 Beta** for the Galaxy S26 series on May 13, 2026, starting in Germany, the UK, South Korea, and the US, with India and Poland joining on May 26. The stable rollout is expected at Samsung's second Unpacked event of the year, around July 22, alongside the Galaxy Z Fold 8 and Z Flip 8.

Since One UI 8.5 already delivered the big visual overhaul, One UI 9 is more of a refinement and feature-expansion release. It brings expanded Galaxy AI tools, thicker brightness/volume sliders, a redesigned lock screen media player with waveform animation, floating app bubbles, a system-level contacts picker, and Parental Controls moved into their own dedicated Settings section — all riding on top of Android 17's platform changes.

> Samsung hasn't published an official eligibility list yet. The list below is based on Samsung's confirmed 4–7 year update policy plus early Android 17 firmware leaks, so treat it as a strong working list rather than gospel until Samsung confirms it.

### Devices Missing Out

The **Galaxy S22 series**, **Galaxy S21 FE**, and **Galaxy Z Fold 4** have hit the end of their major OS update cycles and will **not** receive One UI 9 — they'll stay on quarterly security updates instead.

## How to Join the Beta

1. Open **Samsung Members** and sign in with your Samsung account.
2. Tap the **One UI 9 Beta Programme** banner.
3. Go to **Settings → Software Update** and download the build.
4. Report bugs through Samsung Members to help shape the stable release.

Keep an eye on Samsung Members for the official region-by-region and device-by-device rollout schedule as it firms up over the coming months.
