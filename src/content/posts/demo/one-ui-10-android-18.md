## Why There's No Device List Yet

Let's be upfront: as of today, **Android 17 itself is still in beta** — it hasn't reached stable release. Google has confirmed platform stability and a "this summer" timeline for Pixel devices, but no fixed date. Samsung's One UI 9, which is built on Android 17, is only a few beta builds deep on the Galaxy S26 series.

That means there is currently **no Android 18**, no announced codename, no feature list, and no Samsung "One UI 9.5" or "One UI 10" beta program. Anyone publishing a "confirmed device list" for it right now is guessing — at best from naming patterns, at worst making it up for clicks. This post is the former: an honest, clearly-labeled guess based on precedent, not a leak roundup.

## What the Pattern Suggests

Samsung's last two cycles give us the only real signal we have:

- **One UI 8 → One UI 8.5**: Samsung inserted a mid-cycle ".5" release (based on an Android 16 QPR build) instead of jumping straight to a new major version — a change from its older one-major-update-per-year habit.
- **One UI 9**: followed as the next annual major release, built on Android 17.

If that ".5 then full number" rhythm holds, the next release after One UI 9 could land as **One UI 9.5** (an Android 17 QPR-based refinement, likely around Q4 2026/Q1 2027) before a numbered **One UI 10** arrives alongside whatever Google calls its next annual release — informally "Android 18" — sometime in mid-to-late 2027. None of this is confirmed; it's just the rhythm Samsung has shown twice now.

## What Would Likely Carry Over

Based purely on how Samsung has handled the last few cycles, a future One UI 9.5/10 device list would probably:

- **Lead with the newest flagship** — whatever ships as the next Galaxy S-series (S27?) or foldable generation would get it first, out of the box.
- **Drop devices that maxed out at One UI 9** — phones like the Galaxy S23 series are expected to get One UI 9 as their final major OS update, so they'd likely be absent from the next list.
- **Follow the same 4–7 year tiering** — flagships near the top of their promised update window, A/M/F-series budget phones near the bottom.

## The Honest Bottom Line

Until Android 17 stable actually ships and Samsung confirms what comes next, treat any specific "One UI 10 eligible devices" list you see online as speculation dressed up as fact. The real list won't exist until Samsung opens a beta program for it — realistically not before late 2026 at the earliest.
